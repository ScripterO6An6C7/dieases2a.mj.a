Definitions of Diseases

Common Disease Definitions

Flu
The flu (influenza) is a contagious respiratory illness caused by viruses that infect the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs. It can cause mild to severe illness, and at times can lead to death.
Cancer
Cancer is a group of diseases involving abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body. There are more than 100 different types of cancer.
Diabetes
Diabetes is a disease that occurs when your blood glucose, also called blood sugar, levels are too high. There are two main types: Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes.
Malaria
Malaria is a mosquito-borne infectious disease that affects humans and other animals. It is caused by a parasite that infects red blood cells.
